The Beatles

The Beatles's first album

Please Please Me, The Beatles' first album, was recorded during a 12-hour session on February 11, 1963. The setting was EMI Studios in London, where John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr worked under the guidance of producer George Martin.

The Beatles's most popular album

Abbey Road is the eleventh studio album by the English rock band the Beatles, released on 26 September 1969. It is the last album the group recorded, although Let It Be was the last album completed before the band's break-up in April 1970

The Beatles's popular songs

The Beatles spent 14 consecutive weeks at No. 1 on the Hot 100 in 1964. “I Want to Hold Your Hand” ruled for seven weeks from Feb. 1 to March 14, and was replaced by “She Loves You,” which topped the chart for two weeks. In 1986 the Guinness Book of Records declared 'Yesterday' the most recorded song of all time. There were 1600 versions then and this figure has risen to more than 2000
